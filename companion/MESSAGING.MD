## 2. Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1: Honest Curation

```yaml
pillar_1:
  name: "Honest Curation"
  strategic_claim: "Every wine in our catalog is here because someone chose it — not because it met a volume threshold or paid for placement"
  
  proof_points:
    - "Every SKU is evaluated against a defined quality and value standard before joining the catalog"
    - "We remove wines that no longer meet the standard — the catalog size reflects curation, not accumulation"
    - "We never run ads for wines that aren't in stock — inventory integrity is non-negotiable"
  
  approved_phrases:
    - "Hand-selected"
    - "Every bottle earns its place"
    - "We wouldn't sell it if we wouldn't recommend it"
    - "Chosen, not aggregated"
    - "No filler. No paid placement."
  
  by_persona:
    Explorer:
      angle: "We pick so you can discover — every wine here has a story worth knowing"
      copy_example: "We'd only add a new Burgundy to our catalog if we'd stake our recommendation on it. This one cleared the bar."
    Gifter:
      angle: "When you buy a gift here, you're buying a recommendation, not a guess"
      copy_example: "Every wine here has been chosen by someone who would stand behind it at a dinner party."
    Loyalist:
      angle: "You trust us to get it right. We earn that trust by saying no to wines that don't deserve your cellar."
      copy_example: "We passed on five Barolos before we found one worth telling you about."
    Collector:
      angle: "No compromise. No filler. The catalog reflects that."
      copy_example: "We hold every SKU to the same standard. This one made the cut."
    Deal_Seeker:
      angle: "A deal on a wine we believe in is a real deal"
      copy_example: "15% off, and we'd still drink this on a Tuesday without the discount."
  
  by_funnel_stage:
    awareness: "We only carry wines we'd drink ourselves"
    consideration: "Every wine in our catalog passed our quality standard — here's what that means for this bottle"
    conversion: "This wine earned its place. Here's why it belongs in yours."
  
  do_not_say:
    - "'One of the best wines we've ever tasted' — too superlative without proof"
    - "'Award-winning' without citing the specific award (see approved claims)"
    - "'Curated selection' without substance — the proof is in the specific story"
```

### Pillar 2: Knowledge Without Pretension

```yaml
pillar_2:
  name: "Knowledge Without Pretension"
  strategic_claim: "We know wine deeply and share that knowledge freely — because an informed customer is a loyal one"
  
  proof_points:
    - "Region guides, drinking window notes, and food pairings included with every product"
    - "Educational content published monthly — we give away what others charge for"
    - "We write for curious people, not sommeliers — knowledge without gatekeeping"
  
  approved_phrases:
    - "Here's what makes this special"
    - "What you should know before you open it"
    - "The honest guide to [varietal/region]"
    - "No jargon — just what matters"
    - "The kind of thing your sommelier friend would tell you"
  
  by_persona:
    Explorer:
      angle: "We'll teach you everything you want to know — and nothing you don't"
      copy_example: "Barolo can be intimidating. We wrote the guide we wish we'd had."
    Loyalist:
      angle: "We respect your depth. We'll match it."
      copy_example: "You know Burgundy. This 2022 Gevrey-Chambertin from [producer] adds something the village's reputation doesn't always deliver: precision."
    Collector:
      angle: "Vintage intelligence you can act on — not tasting note padding"
      copy_example: "The 2019 vintage in Barolo produced a structural wine built for 10+ years in the cellar. Here's what that means for this bottle specifically."
    Gifter:
      angle: "We'll give you exactly what to say when they open it"
      copy_example: "Château Margaux is the kind of name that needs no explanation. Here's what to add that will."
  
  do_not_say:
    - "'Complex' without saying what makes it complex"
    - "'Smooth' as a primary descriptor — meaningless without context"
    - "'Pairs with anything' — the opposite of useful"
    - "Wine vocabulary that makes the reader feel ignorant rather than curious"
```

### Pillar 3: Right Wine, Right Customer

```yaml
pillar_3:
  name: "Right Wine, Right Customer"
  strategic_claim: "A Collector and a Gifter are buying from the same catalog for completely different reasons — we talk to them differently"
  
  proof_points:
    - "Persona-native copy across all campaigns — no one-size-fits-all messaging"
    - "Recommendations driven by purchase history, varietal affinity, and occasion — not just popularity"
    - "Suppression rules protect relationships — we never pitch a Loyalist a discount on a wine they've bought at full price"
  
  approved_phrases:
    - "Made for [occasion]"
    - "The right wine for [this moment/this person/this table]"
    - "We know what you're looking for [implied — don't say this directly]"
  
  by_persona:
    Explorer:
      angle: "We match your curiosity to the right region, the right producer, the right price point"
    Gifter:
      angle: "We know what impresses without requiring expertise to explain"
    Loyalist:
      angle: "We know your preferences well enough to only bring you what's worth your attention"
    Collector:
      angle: "We know which allocations you should be considering — and we'll tell you why"
  
  do_not_say:
    - "'Perfect for everyone' — the opposite of our positioning"
    - "'Great for any occasion' — lazy; specificity is the product"
```

## 5. Anti-Hallucination Rules

```yaml
anti_hallucination:
  
  wine_specific_prohibition_list:
    never_generate:
      - "Wine press scores — only use from approved claims library with source + date"
      - "Winemaker biographical details not in product catalog"
      - "Production quantities without verified source"
      - "Drinking window guidance — pull from product catalog only; do not generate"
      - "Vintage condition descriptions ('a warm, dry summer...') without a verified source"
      - "Appellation or region classification details — use product catalog data"
      - "Anything about a winemaker's personal life, beliefs, or history unless in catalog"
    
    if_in_doubt: "Omit the claim. A wine described without an invented score is more trustworthy than one with a fabricated '92 points'."
  
  sourcing_requirements:
    must_come_from_approved_claims_library:
      - "Press scores (all four fields required: publication, score, vintage, date)"
      - "Awards and certifications"
      - "Winemaker quotes"
      - "Comparative claims"
      - "Production quantity claims"
    
    must_come_from_product_catalog:
      - "Vintage year (always — never generate)"
      - "Tasting notes (use catalog tasting notes; add to them; do not replace them)"
      - "Drinking window (pull exactly; do not estimate)"
      - "Producer/winery name (correct spelling from catalog)"
      - "Varietal and region (from catalog)"
      - "Price (always pull live; never generate)"
    
    must_come_from_campaign_brief:
      - "Discount codes and percentages"
      - "Offer end dates"
      - "Featured SKUs for the campaign"
  
  claim_review_routing:
    agent_detects_desired_claim_not_in_library:
      step_1: "Omit claim from copy"
      step_2: "Flag to human: 'Wine score or claim needed for [campaign] not found in approved library: [description]'"
      step_3: "Human verifies and adds to library, or removes from brief"
      step_4: "Do not publish until the claim is verified or removed"
```

## 6. Competitive Claim Rules

```yaml
competitive_claims:
  
  permitted_wine_comparisons:
    - "Appellation-level comparisons (factual — e.g., Napa vs. Burgundy regional characteristics)"
    - "Vintage-level comparisons between regions (factual — based on documented conditions)"
    - "Style comparisons ('this is more structured than approachable — built for the cellar')"
  
  prohibited_wine_comparisons:
    - "Comparing our selection to a specific competitor's selection without substantiation"
    - "Claiming our prices are lower than a competitor's without verified current pricing"
    - "Disparaging a specific producer's wine quality — the wine community is small"
    - "Implying a competitor carries inferior wines without evidence"
  
  agent_rule: "Wine tasting is subjective. Never use preference language as a comparative claim against a competitor. Stick to verifiable facts — region, vintage, production method."
```
